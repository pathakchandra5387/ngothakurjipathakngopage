About Thakur Ji Pathak

Thakur Ji Pathak (1968 – 1994) was an Indian social activist and politician. He was the National General Secretary of Janata Dal. Born in Narahi, Ballia, Uttar Pradesh, he graduated from K.M.P.M Inter College, Jamshedpur. He joined the Janata Party in the 1980s and later Janata Dal in 1988. Known for his support of women's rights and helping poor people, he was close to former Prime Minister Chandra Shekhar. Tragically, he was murdered by criminals in front of the Jamshedpur High Court in 1994 at the age of 26.

About the Society

The Shaheed Thakur Ji Pathak Welfare Society was founded in 1995 in memory of Thakur Ji Pathak. The organization actively supports needy and poor people. It facilitates the Shaheed Thakurji Pathak Welfare Award annually. Based in Jamshedpur, Jharkhand, the society carries forward his mission of social welfare.

Our Activities

  • Distributing food supplies to needy families on death anniversaries.
  • Providing masks and essential items to disabled people during crises like COVID-19.
  • Organizing tributes and commemorative programs to honor Thakur Ji Pathak's legacy.
  • Awarding the Shaheed Thakurji Pathak Welfare Award to deserving individuals.
  • Supporting marginalized communities and promoting social responsibility.
